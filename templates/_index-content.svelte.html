Improved infrastructure

Pawlenty's administration struggled with complaints of unattended potholes and neglected bridges as controversies over infrastructure maintenance raged, especially centered around the collapse of the I-35W Bridge in 2007. Under Dayton, increased spending on roads improved conditions on Minnesota's major thoroughfares and decreased urban congestion.

Dayton also convened task forces to address technological infrastructure throughout the state, ushering in an area of expanded broadband access, even while some digital gaps remain.

Reductions in crime

Matching national trends, crime rates in Minnesota have steadily declined over the past two decades.

In Minneapolis, violent crime spiked then fell dramatically during the Pawlety era and has remained flatter in recent years.

However, the number of suspects killed by police in Minnesota reached 20-year highs as the issue drew a national spotlight.
