How Minnesota has changed under three governors

By Jeff Hargarten and Matt DeLong • Star Tribune

December 23, 2018

Following an ignominious tenure as a U.S. Senator, Mark Dayton won by a hair’s breadth in 2010’s heavily-contested gubernatorial election as a divisive figure in Minnesota’s politics.

But time and circumstance were kind to Dayton as the state survived and thrived through recession and difficulty, and he claimed a more decisive victory for reelection in 2014. He now leaves office having boasted relatively high approval ratings throughout his administration.

As Mark Dayton’s eight-year term as Minnesota’s governor comes to an end, here’s a look back at how the state has changed over the course of his administration and those of his predecessors.

A robust state economy

While Minnesota weathered the Great Recession's storm better than most states, various economic woes still marred Pawlenty's governorship. But by the time Dayton took office, Minnesota's economy started to strongly rebound and has continued to grow.
