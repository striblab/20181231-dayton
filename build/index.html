Several ways Minnesota has changed under Mark Dayton

By Jeff Hargarten and Matt DeLong • Star Tribune

January 4, 2019

As Mark Dayton nears the end of his tenure as governor, one thing is certain: a lot has happened across Minnesota the past eight years. The job market recovered from a deep recession, but many Minnesotans continue to struggle. The cost of college tuition crept ever higher, while crime rates fell. The state’s budget went from years of deficits to sizeable surpluses.

To be sure, the governor can’t take all of the credit (or the blame) for everything that happens in the state. But as a new administration prepares to take the reins, we dug into the data to measure how Minnesota has changed under Dayton’s watch.
